# Production Playbook

## Principle

Audio and timing come first. Write the narration before the visuals, because
scene duration and editing rhythm should follow the voice.

## Production Phases

1. Script
   - Write the hook, beats, and ending.
   - Keep one idea per scene.
   - Read it out loud before spending credits.

2. Keyframes
   - Generate stills first.
   - Keep text unreadable or out of frame.
   - Use a stable style anchor across all images.

3. Animation
   - Describe action, not the whole image again.
   - Use one camera move per clip.
   - Always specify ambient audio.

4. Assembly
   - Match each clip to its narration segment.
   - Mix generated ambient audio under the voice.
   - Add captions only when they help the platform and do not block the image.

## Batch Strategy

Vary one thing at a time:

- Hook framing.
- Visual metaphor.
- Model choice.
- Camera move.
- Narration voice.

Do not vary everything at once, or you will not know what improved the result.

## When To Stop

Stop generation and revise the spec when:

- The first frame does not communicate the topic.
- A key prop is warped or illegible.
- Text appears accidentally.
- The style changes between scenes.
- The narration is too dense for the duration.

## Useful Formats In This Kit

- Narrated philosophy/history episode.
- Dark romantic grimoire/language essay.
- Mythic magic-origin story.
- Ambient magical loop.
